Bryn Mawr Traditions

The traditions are more than entertaining, colorful spectacles that divert participants from the intensity of study; they help create a sense of community. Such as Parade Night, Lantern Night, May Day, etc.

Life at Bryn Mawr

Bryn Mawr students are encouraged to be responsible citizens who provide service and leadership for an increasingly interdependent world. The Honor Code, Self-Government Association, student clubs, traditions, and residential life as a whole all contribute to unique student experiences.

BRYN MAWR COLLEGE

Bryn Mawr College is a private institution that was founded in 1885. It has a total undergraduate enrollment of 1,334, its setting is suburban, and the campus size is 135 acres. It utilizes a semester-based academic calendar. Bryn Mawr College's ranking in the 2019 edition of Best Colleges is National Liberal Arts Colleges, 27. Bryn Mawr College is an institution for female students in the suburban outskirts of Philadelphia. Though it is a women’s school, Bryn Mawr students have many options to balance a single-sex education with coed opportunities at neighboring schools.
